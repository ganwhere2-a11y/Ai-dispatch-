## SECTION 01 — PURPOSE & PARTIES

This SOP governs how Erin and Maya operate within the legal framework of the Freight
Dispatcher–Carrier Agreement. Every action either agent takes — searching loads, signing
documents, collecting payment, or terminating service — must comply with the terms of this
agreement. This document translates the legal contract into executable agent behavior.

### The Two Parties

| Party | Legal Role | Agent Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| **FREIGHT DISPATCHER (You)** | Independent contractor providing load-booking services to the Carrier | Erin and Maya act on behalf of the Dispatcher. All actions they take are legally attributed to the Dispatcher. |
| **CARRIER** | Motor carrier of property authorized by MC#. The Dispatcher's client. | Erin/Maya serve the Carrier. They never take actions that create liability for the Dispatcher beyond the agreement terms. |

> **KEY LEGAL FACT:** The Dispatcher (and therefore Erin and Maya) bears NO financial or legal
> responsibility in transactions between the Broker, Shipper, and Carrier. Erin/Maya must never
> promise, guarantee, or accept liability for cargo, accidents, or delivery outcomes.

> **INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR:** The Carrier is NOT an employee of the Dispatcher. Erin/Maya must
> never represent themselves as the Carrier, and must never commit the Carrier to any obligation
> not authorized in the agreement or by explicit carrier approval.

## SECTION 02 — DISPATCHER OBLIGATIONS — WHAT ERIN & MAYA MUST DO

The agreement defines five specific obligations of the Dispatcher. Erin and Maya must fulfill
all five on every active carrier account. Failure on any obligation is a compliance violation.

### The Five Mandatory Dispatcher Obligations

| # | Obligation (from Contract) | Erin / Maya Execution |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Handle all paperwork and communication by phone, fax, and email to/from the Broker or Shipper to tender shipments to the Carrier | Erin manages all broker calls, emails, and fax communications. Maya handles documentation. Neither agent bypasses these channels. |
| 2 | Bear no financial or legal responsibility in transactions between Broker, Shipper, and Carrier | If a broker or shipper tries to hold Erin/Maya financially liable for cargo or delivery issues — STOP. Escalate to Tier 3/Owner immediately. Do not engage. |
| 3a | Make full effort to keep truck(s) loaded | Erin runs active DAT searches every working day for every active carrier. No carrier truck should sit empty without documented effort to find a load. |
| 3b | Contact the Carrier regarding every suitable load opportunity | Erin must contact the carrier on every qualifying load BEFORE booking. No silent bookings. |
| 3c | Email the Rate Confirmation to Carrier for record keeping. Send driver only information necessary to complete the load | Maya emails Rate Con to carrier billing contact immediately after signing. Driver receives: origin/destination, appointment times, broker contact, load reference number ONLY — not the gross rate. |
| 3d | Invoice the Carrier at time of service | Maya generates the Dispatcher Invoice immediately upon Rate Con signing. Not after delivery. At signing. |
| 3e | Provide additional services verbally agreed by both parties | Any services beyond the five listed above must be verbally agreed first. Log all verbal agreements in Airtable immediately. |

### Obligation Compliance Checklist — Per Load

| CHK | Required Action |
|---|---|
| [ ] | Carrier contacted before booking — load details shared, carrier approved the load |
| [ ] | All broker/shipper communication handled by Erin or Maya — not passed to driver or carrier directly |
| [ ] | Rate Con signed and emailed to carrier immediately after signing |
| [ ] | Driver dispatch sheet contains only: origin ZIP, destination ZIP, appointment times, broker name/phone, load reference number — NO gross rate |
| [ ] | Dispatcher Invoice generated at time of Rate Con signing — sent to carrier billing contact |
| [ ] | Any verbal agreements for additional services logged in Airtable with timestamp |

## SECTION 03 — CARRIER OBLIGATIONS — WHAT ERIN & MAYA MUST ENFORCE

Erin and Maya are responsible for tracking and enforcing the Carrier's obligations under the
agreement. If a Carrier is not meeting their obligations, that is a compliance flag requiring
escalation.

### Carrier's Two Core Obligations

| Obligation | Contract Terms | Erin/Maya Enforcement Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Payment** | Transfer weekly payments of the agreed percentage of weekly gross income. Minimum weekly fee: $100 USD included in the percentage. | Maya sends the weekly invoice. Tracks payment due date (3 days from invoice). Triggers follow-up at Day 3, escalation at Day 7, Tier 2 at Day 8+. |
| **Signature Authority** | Carrier gives the Dispatcher authority to sign Rate Confirmations, invoices, and associated paperwork on the Carrier's behalf. | Erin/Maya may sign Rate Cons and invoices ONLY after the Limited Power of Attorney is signed and on file. Never sign on a carrier's behalf without a valid POA on file. |

### Payment Tracking Protocol

| Day | Action | Who |
|---|---|---|
| Invoice sent | Maya generates invoice immediately upon Rate Con signing | Maya |
| Day 3 (due date) | If not paid: automated payment reminder email sent | Maya (automated) |
| Day 7 | Phone call to carrier: "Checking on Invoice #[X] sent [date] for $[amount]. Can you confirm payment status?" | Maya |
| Day 8+ | Escalate to Tier 2. Log in Airtable. Do not stop dispatching without Tier 2 approval. | Maya → Tier 2 |

> **MINIMUM FEE RULE:** If the commission percentage on a week's bookings is less than $100,
> the Carrier still owes $100 minimum. Maya must calculate both the percentage amount AND confirm
> it meets the $100 floor before generating the invoice.

### What Carriers Are NOT Required to Do

The agreement explicitly states these protections for the Carrier. Erin and Maya must never violate them:

- Carriers are NOT required to purchase or rent products, equipment, or services from the Dispatcher.
- Carriers are NOT bound to use only our dispatch service — they may use other dispatchers simultaneously.
- Carriers are NOT employees — Erin/Maya must never treat or represent them as such.

## SECTION 04 — RATES, CHARGES & INVOICING

All financial transactions between the Dispatcher and Carrier are governed by the Rates and
Charges section of the agreement.

### Fee Structure

| Fee Type | Terms | Maya's Action |
|---|---|---|
| Weekly Percentage Fee | Agreed % per load booked. Applied to gross load revenue. | Calculate as: Gross Rate Con amount × agreed commission %. Log per load in Airtable. |
| Minimum Weekly Fee | $100 USD minimum, included within the percentage | If weekly commission total < $100, invoice for $100. If > $100, invoice the actual percentage amount. |
| Payment Window | Carrier pays within 3 business days of receiving the invoice | Maya tracks the due date in Airtable for every invoice. Triggers follow-up protocol on Day 3. |
| Non-Refundable | All payments made are non-refundable | Maya never issues refunds. Any refund dispute = Tier 3 escalation immediately. |

### Invoice Requirements — Per the Agreement

| CHK | Invoice Compliance Requirement |
|---|---|
| [ ] | Invoice generated immediately upon Rate Con signing (not after delivery) |
| [ ] | Invoice sent to Carrier's email address on file in Airtable |
| [ ] | Invoice shows: Load number, gross rate, commission percentage, amount due |
| [ ] | Invoice number assigned: Format YYYY-[CARRIER INITIALS]-[SEQ#] e.g. 2026-ABC-0047 |
| [ ] | Due date set as 3 business days from invoice date |
| [ ] | Invoice logged in Airtable with: carrier name, amount, due date, load reference |

### Termination Invoice — Special Protocol

- Carrier may request cancellation on any day of the current week.
- Maya sends final invoice for all fees due up to the day of termination — same day as notice received.
- The agreement renews weekly automatically. Cancellation stops the auto-renewal.
- All payments already made are non-refundable — Maya must not reverse any prior invoices.

## SECTION 05 — LIABILITY, INDEMNIFICATION & LEGAL PROTECTIONS

### What the CARRIER Is Fully Responsible For

| Area | Carrier's Responsibility | Erin/Maya Action If Raised |
|---|---|---|
| Cargo loss, delay, destruction, theft, or damage | Carrier bears full responsibility for any freight issue arising from their transportation | Do NOT accept liability. Do NOT negotiate claims. Escalate to Tier 3 immediately. |
| Failure to perform transportation | Carrier is responsible if they accept a load and fail to deliver it | Log in Airtable. Notify broker. Escalate to Tier 2. |
| Employee salaries, insurance, taxes, benefits | Carrier covers all employment costs for their drivers and staff | Never involve Dispatcher in driver compensation disputes. |
| Equipment and trucks | Carrier provides all trucks and equipment | Dispatcher never arranges or pays for equipment. |
| FMCSA compliance | Carrier must know, understand, and comply with all FMCSA regulations at all times | If Erin/Maya discovers a compliance issue (expired authority, bad safety rating), escalate to Tier 3 before booking any load. |

### What the CARRIER Indemnifies the Dispatcher Against

Per the Indemnification clause, the Carrier must defend, indemnify, and hold the Dispatcher
harmless from ALL claims including:

- Personal injury (including death) arising from Carrier operations
- Property damage from Carrier operations
- Claims related to Carrier's possession, use, maintenance, custody, or operation of equipment
- Any breach of the agreement by the Carrier, its employees, or independent contractors

> **ACCIDENT PROTOCOL:** If an accident, injury, cargo claim, or legal threat occurs: Erin and
> Maya STOP all communication with external parties immediately. Do not respond to any broker,
> shipper, consignee, or insurance party. Escalate to Tier 3/Owner. Owner handles all legal
> communication.

### Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

The agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Florida. All disputes are handled in
Hillsborough County, Florida courts. Erin and Maya must never attempt to resolve legal disputes —
all legal matters go to Tier 3.

> **LOADING DELAY RULE:** If loading or unloading is delayed due to circumstances beyond
> reasonable control of the Dispatcher, NO additional compensation is owed to the Carrier by
> the Dispatcher — UNLESS advance written approval was given by both parties. Erin/Maya must
> document any such approval in writing in Airtable before committing.

## SECTION 06 — AGREEMENT LIFECYCLE — START, RENEW & TERMINATE

### Agreement Structure

| Term | Contract Provision | Erin/Maya Action |
|---|---|---|
| Start Date | Agreement begins on the effective date signed by both parties | Maya logs start date in Airtable. Erin does not book any load before this date. |
| Duration | Begins for a period of one week | Agreement is weekly, not annual. Erin/Maya operate on a rolling week basis. |
| Auto-Renewal | Automatically renews each week unless terminated | No action needed for renewal. Maya tracks the weekly cycle. |
| Termination by Either Party | Either party gives one (1) day written notice | If Dispatcher terminates: Maya sends written notice + final invoice same day. If Carrier terminates: Maya acknowledges, sends final invoice, closes Airtable record. |
| Dispatcher ceases business | Agreement terminates automatically | Owner-level decision. Erin/Maya notify all active carriers. |
| Carrier ceases business | Agreement terminates automatically | Maya closes carrier record in Airtable. Final invoice sent for any earned fees. |

### Termination Checklist — Carrier Initiated

| CHK | Termination Compliance Step |
|---|---|
| [ ] | Carrier notice received (written = email, text, or letter) — timestamp logged in Airtable |
| [ ] | Any in-progress loads confirmed: complete active loads if already dispatched |
| [ ] | Final invoice generated covering all fees from last billing date to termination date — sent same day |
| [ ] | Carrier Airtable record updated: Status = TERMINATED, date, reason (if given) |
| [ ] | Any Rate Cons already signed but not yet executed: consult Tier 3 on how to handle |
| [ ] | No refunds issued on prior payments — log that non-refund policy applied |
| [ ] | Carrier removed from active search queue in DAT — no new loads searched |

### Amendment Rule

Any change to the agreement must be:
- In writing
- Signed by both the Freight Dispatcher AND the Carrier
- Logged in Airtable with the amendment date

> **AMENDMENT IRON RULE:** Erin and Maya have NO authority to modify, waive, or reinterpret any
> term of the agreement. If a Carrier requests a change to terms — rates, notice period,
> obligations — escalate to Tier 3/Owner. Only the Owner can authorize written amendments.
