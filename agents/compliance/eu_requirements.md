## 1. EU GDP — Good Distribution Practice Guidelines for Medical Freight

### What GDP Is

**Good Distribution Practice (GDP)** is the EU regulatory framework governing the wholesale distribution of medicinal products for human use. It is mandated under **EU Directive 2001/83/EC** and enforced through detailed guidelines published by the European Commission (2013/C 68/01).

GDP applies to any entity in the distribution chain of medicines and medical products — including freight carriers transporting pharmaceutical goods across EU member states.

### What GDP Requires for Freight Carriers

**Transportation and Distribution:**
- All medicinal products must be transported in conditions that preserve product integrity
- Temperature-sensitive products (refrigerated or frozen) must be maintained within defined temperature ranges at all times during transport
- Vehicles used for pharmaceutical transport must be qualified for the intended use
- Temperature mapping studies must be conducted on vehicle compartments used for controlled temperature transport

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Written procedures (SOPs) must exist for all GDP-relevant activities
- Temperature monitoring data must be available for each shipment
- Deviations from required conditions must be documented and investigated
- Delivery records must include: date of supply, name and address of recipient, product description, quantity, batch number, expiry date

**Personnel and Training:**
- Carriers must ensure personnel handling pharmaceutical freight are trained in GDP principles
- A designated "Responsible Person" must oversee GDP compliance

**Quality Management:**
- GDP carriers should have a quality management system (QMS) or be able to demonstrate equivalent controls
- Self-inspection/audits required periodically

### GDP Relevance for Dry Van Medical Freight

Our operation dispatches **dry van** freight, which means:
- We do not carry temperature-sensitive pharmaceuticals requiring refrigeration
- We do carry: medical devices, hospital supplies, surgical instruments, PPE, diagnostic equipment — these are generally not GDP-regulated in the same way as medicines
- However: if a shipper has medical products that ARE GDP-relevant (any finished pharmaceutical product), the carrier must demonstrate GDP-compliant capabilities
- **Erin's rule for EU:** Before booking any load that includes finished pharmaceutical products in the EU, confirm the carrier holds GDP certification or equivalent and flag to Compliance Agent.

## 2. CMR Documentation — Convention on the Contract for the International Carriage of Goods by Road

### What CMR Is

The **CMR Convention** (Convention on the Contract for the International Carriage of Goods by Road, 1956) is an international treaty governing road freight contracts when the origin or destination is in a signatory country. All major EU countries are CMR signatories, as is the UK.

### The CMR Consignment Note

The CMR consignment note is the primary transport document for international road freight in Europe. It serves as:
- A receipt for the goods
- Evidence of the contract of carriage
- A document of title (limited)

**Required CMR fields (Article 6 of CMR Convention):**
- Date of consignment note and place of issue
- Sender's name and address
- Carrier's name and address
- Consignee's name and address
- Place and date of taking over the goods
- Place designated for delivery
- Description of the goods (nature, packaging, number of packages, special marks)
- Gross weight or quantity
- Charges relating to carriage (freight, surcharges, customs duties)
- Instructions for customs and other formalities
- Statement that the carriage is subject to CMR (if applicable)

**CMR vs. USA BOL:**

| Feature | USA Bill of Lading | EU CMR Note |
|---|---|---|
| Legal basis | UCC Article 7 | CMR Convention 1956 |
| Copies required | Typically 1-2 | 3 originals (carrier, sender, accompanies goods) |
| Liability cap | Varies | 8.33 SDR per kg of gross weight |
| Carrier liability | Negligence standard | Presumed liable unless force majeure |
| Time limit for claims | Varies by state | 1 year (3 years for intentional fault) |

### CMR Liability for Medical Freight

Because CMR limits carrier liability to **8.33 SDR per kg** (approximately €10-11/kg at current rates), medical device loads with high value relative to weight are significantly under-covered by CMR default limits. Shippers should declare special interest in delivery (Article 26) to obtain higher coverage, or obtain supplemental cargo insurance.

## 3. Cabotage Rules — What a US/Canada Carrier Can and Cannot Do in the EU

### Definition of Cabotage

Cabotage refers to the carriage of goods between two points within the same country or trading bloc by a carrier from outside that country/bloc.

### EU Cabotage Regulations (Regulation EC 1072/2009)

**What is allowed:**
- A non-EU carrier (including US/Canada) can perform **up to 3 cabotage operations within 7 days** after completing an international delivery into the EU
- Cabotage operations must be within the same EU member state as the final delivery of the international load
- After completing allowed cabotage, the carrier must leave the EU before performing more

**What is NOT allowed:**
- Unlimited cabotage within the EU by non-EU carriers
- Cabotage in a different EU member state than where the international load was delivered
- Permanent or regular cabotage by non-EU carriers

### Practical Implications

For our business model dispatching US-based dry van carriers:
- A US carrier delivering from the US to Germany can do up to 3 loads entirely within Germany before returning
- That same carrier cannot then move freight from Germany to France on a domestic French load (that would be French cabotage by a non-EU carrier, which is not permitted)
- Cross-border loads within the EU (e.g., Germany to France) are **NOT cabotage** — they are international loads and are permitted

**Erin's guidance for EU phase:** Most loads we dispatch will be international (US/Canada to EU or EU to US/Canada). Intra-EU cabotage is a secondary capability. Flag any cabotage requests to Compliance Agent before booking.

## 4. EU Carrier Licensing

### Operator's Licence (EU Road Transport)

To operate commercial road freight within the EU, carriers must hold an **EU Community Licence** issued under Regulation EC 1071/2009 and EC 1072/2009. Non-EU carriers do not hold EU Community Licences but may operate under bilateral agreements or transit rules.

### EUR.1 Movement Certificate

The **EUR.1 certificate** is a customs document used to prove the origin of goods when exporting to countries with which the EU has a preferential trade agreement. It allows goods of EU origin to be imported at preferential (reduced or zero) tariff rates.

**When EUR.1 is needed:**
- Exporting EU-origin medical goods to countries with EU preferential agreements
- Issued by customs authorities of the exporting EU country
- The shipper/exporter must apply; the carrier presents it at customs

**EUR.1 is NOT required:**
- For goods without preferential origin status
- When using a REX (Registered Exporter) statement instead
- For goods below certain value thresholds (use origin declaration instead)

### TIR Carnet

The **TIR Carnet** (Transports Internationaux Routiers) is an international customs document that allows goods to be transported across international borders with reduced customs formalities. It acts as a guarantee that customs duties will be paid if goods are not properly re-exported.

**TIR relevance:**
- Useful for loads transiting through non-EU countries (e.g., Turkey, Ukraine)
- Administered by the IRU (International Road Transport Union)
- Carrier must be authorized by their national association to use TIR
- Less relevant for direct US-to-EU shipments via air/sea port of entry

## 5. EU Road Transport Regulations — EC 561/2006 (Hours of Service)

### Overview

**Regulation EC 561/2006** sets driving time limits and rest period requirements for commercial vehicle drivers in the EU. It applies to all drivers operating within EU member states, regardless of the carrier's country of registration.

### Key Limits Under EC 561/2006

| Rule | EU (EC 561/2006) | USA (FMCSA) |
|---|---|---|
| Daily driving time | 9 hours (extendable to 10h twice/week) | 11 hours |
| Weekly driving time | 56 hours | 60h/7 days |
| Two-week driving time | 90 hours | 70h/8 days |
| Daily rest | 11 hours (reducible to 9h three times between weekly rests) | 10 consecutive hours |
| Weekly rest | 45 hours (reducible to 24h, compensation within 3 weeks) | 34-hour restart |
| Break after 4.5h driving | 45 minutes (or 15+30) | 30-minute break after 8h |

### Digital Tachograph Requirement

EU vehicles over 3.5 tonnes GVW must be equipped with a **digital tachograph** (smart tachograph as of 2019 for new vehicles). This is the EU's equivalent of the ELD mandate.

**For US carriers operating in the EU:**
- Must use a tachograph-equipped vehicle or install a tachograph
- Driver must have a tachograph card (issued by the driver's home country)
- Non-EU drivers typically use **temporary tachograph cards** for short EU operations
- EU HOS rules apply from the moment the driver is operating within EU jurisdiction

## 6. Temperature Monitoring Requirements for Pharma Freight

### WHO and EU GDP Standards

For any pharmaceutical or temperature-sensitive medical freight:

**Required temperature ranges:**
| Product Type | Required Range | Monitoring Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Ambient pharmaceuticals | 15°C – 25°C | Continuous data logger |
| Refrigerated (cold chain) | 2°C – 8°C | Continuous data logger + alarm |
| Deep frozen | -20°C ± 5°C | Continuous logger + dual probe |
| Room temperature (non-pharma) | < 30°C with < 70% RH | Recommended but not mandated |

**Monitoring documentation requirements:**
- Temperature data log must be downloaded and reviewed after each shipment
- Any excursion (temperature out of range) must be documented in a deviation report
- Deviation reports must assess impact on product quality
- Logs must be retained for at least 1 year (longer if product shelf life requires)

**Note for dry van medical freight:** Our standard loads (medical devices, hospital supplies, surgical instruments) are typically NOT temperature-sensitive. However, some hospital supply chain clients may include pharmaceutical products. Always ask: "Does this load include any temperature-sensitive items?" before booking.

### UK (Post-Brexit)

The UK is **no longer part of the EU** and operates under its own regulatory regime following Brexit (January 31, 2020; transition ended December 31, 2020).

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Carrier licensing | **Goods Vehicle Operator's Licence** (O-licence) required for UK-based carriers. Non-UK carriers need appropriate bilateral agreement authority. |
| UK-EU trade | Governed by the **UK-EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement (TCA)** |
| Customs | Customs declarations required at UK-EU border. Use **CHIEF/CDS** system (HMRC). |
| Hours of service | UK retained EC 561/2006 rules post-Brexit; enforced by DVSA |
| Tachograph | Digital tachograph still required; UK has its own tachograph cards |
| Import/export documents | Commercial invoice, packing list, customs declaration (C88/E2), commodity codes (UK Tariff) |
| VAT | UK VAT applies; import VAT at point of entry; can be deferred via Postponed VAT Accounting |
| Medical device regulation | UK now uses **UKCA marking** instead of EU CE marking; regulated by MHRA |
| Cabotage | Separate UK cabotage rules under TCA: 2 cabotage operations within 7 days |

**Key UK-EU difference for medical freight:** Medical devices sold in the UK must bear **UKCA marking** (not CE marking) as of deadlines being phased in. This is a product compliance issue for the shipper but Erin should note that UK medical freight may have different documentation requirements than EU medical freight.

## 8. EU Customs Clearance Process for Medical Freight

### Entry to EU (Import)

1. **Customs Declaration:** The importer (or their customs broker) files an **Import Declaration** in the destination country's customs system (e.g., ATLAS in Germany, DELTA-I in Netherlands, DELT@ in France)
2. **Pre-Arrival Notification:** The carrier or freight forwarder submits an **Entry Summary Declaration (ENS)** in the ICS2 system (EU Import Control System 2) at least 1 hour before arrival at EU border for road transport
3. **Document Verification:** Customs checks: commercial invoice, CMR, packing list, EUR.1 or REX statement (if applicable), import permit (for regulated medical products)
4. **Health and Safety Checks:** For certain medical goods, inspection by customs may include coordination with health authorities
5. **Duty Assessment:** Applicable customs duties and VAT calculated; carrier may hold goods pending payment
6. **Release for Free Circulation:** Customs issues release; carrier can proceed to delivery

### Medical Device-Specific Requirements

- **CE Marking:** Medical devices sold in the EU must bear CE marking per EU MDR 2017/745
- **EUDAMED:** EU database for medical devices; importers may need to register devices
- **Import Declaration HS Codes:** Use Chapter 90 (optical, measuring, medical instruments) for most medical devices; Chapter 30 for pharmaceuticals

### Prohibited and Restricted Items

Certain medical products require **import licenses** or are subject to restrictions:
- Controlled substances (narcotic drugs, psychotropics): require DEA/national authority permits
- Radioactive materials: require IAEA/national authority permits
- Blood products: require additional health authority documentation

## 10. Compliance Checklist — Every EU Load

Complete before Erin books any EU-bound or EU-originating load. Log in Airtable under the load record.

- [ ] **1. Carrier EU Operating Authority Confirmed** — Carrier holds EU Community Licence or valid bilateral agreement authority for the specific country of operation.
- [ ] **2. Digital Tachograph Equipped** — Vehicle has certified digital tachograph; driver has valid tachograph card.
- [ ] **3. EU HOS Compliance Confirmed** — Driver's hours available for the load have been verified against EC 561/2006 limits; not a US HOS assumption.
- [ ] **4. CMR Note Prepared** — Three original CMR notes prepared: one retained by sender, one accompanies shipment, one retained by carrier.
- [ ] **5. Entry Summary Declaration (ENS/ICS2)** — Carrier or freight forwarder confirms ENS will be submitted at least 1 hour before EU border arrival.
- [ ] **6. Customs Broker Engaged** — Shipper confirms a licensed EU customs broker is handling import declaration in destination country.
- [ ] **7. Commercial Invoice Complete** — Invoice includes HS tariff codes, country of origin, declared value in EUR, Incoterms, and complete party details.
- [ ] **8. EUR.1 / Rules of Origin** — Checked whether goods qualify for preferential tariff treatment; EUR.1 certificate obtained or REX statement prepared if applicable.
- [ ] **9. Medical Device Compliance** — If load includes medical devices: CE marking verified (EU) or UKCA marking verified (UK); EUDAMED registration checked if applicable.
- [ ] **10. GDP Assessment** — If load includes any finished pharmaceutical products: carrier's GDP certification confirmed; temperature monitoring plan documented.
- [ ] **11. Road Toll Registration** — Carrier has active toll account for countries on route (Toll Collect for Germany, péage for France, etc.).
- [ ] **12. Iron Rule Confirmation** — Load weight ≤ 48,000 lbs; dry van only; no Florida rule not applicable in EU but all other Iron Rules confirmed applicable Iron Rules verified.

**If any item is incomplete:** Erin does NOT book the load. Escalate to Maya if EU expansion is in progress and guidance is needed.
