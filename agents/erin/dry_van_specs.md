# Dry Van Specifications — Erin's Reference

## What Is a Dry Van?

A dry van is a standard enclosed trailer used to haul general freight that doesn't
need temperature control. It's the most common trailer type in trucking.

Dry vans haul:
- Medical supplies and devices (our specialty)
- Household goods (furniture, appliances)
- Building materials (wood, plastic, metals)
- Food products (non-refrigerated)
- General merchandise (retail, warehouse goods)

Dry vans do NOT haul:
- Anything requiring refrigeration (reefer trailer)
- Hazardous materials without special certification (hazmat)
- Oversized or heavy equipment (flatbed)
- Livestock (specialty trailer)
- Bulk liquids (tanker)

## Trailer Dimensions

| Spec | Standard | Small |
|---|---|---|
| Length | 53 feet | 48 feet |
| Height (interior) | 13.6 feet | 12.5 feet |
| Width (interior) | 8.6 feet | 8.6 feet |

Most common: 53-foot trailer.

## Weight Limits

| Item | Weight |
|---|---|
| Trailer itself (tare weight) | 14,000 - 16,000 lbs |
| Max cargo weight (Iron Rule) | 48,000 lbs |
| Typical safe max cargo | 44,000 - 45,000 lbs |
| Total truck + trailer + cargo | 80,000 lbs (federal limit) |

## Pallet Capacity

- Standard large dry van (53ft): **26 pallets**
- Small dry van (48ft): **26 pallets** (tighter fit — not always accepted)
- Standard pallet size: 48" × 40" (GMA pallet)

When a shipper asks about pallet count, always confirm:
1. Are these standard 48×40 pallets?
2. Are they stackable?
3. What is total weight per pallet?

Formula: `total_weight = pallet_count × weight_per_pallet`
Always verify this matches the BOL weight.

## Interstate vs. Intrastate

| Type | Definition | Rules |
|---|---|---|
| Interstate | Crosses state lines OR freight originated in another state | FMCSA federal regulations apply |
| Intrastate | Start and end in same state | State-specific DOT rules apply |

Our business is primarily interstate. For intrastate jobs, verify the specific
state's DOT rules — they sometimes differ from federal FMCSA requirements.

## Medical Freight Notes

Medical supplies are the same as general dry van freight for regulatory purposes
(FDA Class I/II devices, hospital supplies, DME). No special trailer or
certification needed — just a clean, sealed dry van.

Erin's advantage: She knows which carriers handle medical clients well and
which ones deliver on time. This is stored in memory and the decision engine.
