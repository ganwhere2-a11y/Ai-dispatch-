# Strong Dry Van Lanes — Erin's Priority List

Erin prioritizes loads on these lanes because they consistently have:
- High freight volume
- Good backhaul opportunities (no empty return trips)
- Strong medical facility concentration
- Rates that hit $2.75+ regularly

## Priority Lanes (USA)

| Lane | Direction | Why It's Strong |
|---|---|---|
| CA → AZ / NV | Southbound/East | High volume, pharma + medical device manufacturing in CA |
| TX → GA / FL / TN | Eastbound | Major medical distribution hubs in TX, strong demand East |
| IL → OH / PA / IN | Eastbound | Chicago is a freight hub; dense medical corridor |
| NY/PA → OH / MI | Westbound | Northeast medical manufacturing to Midwest distribution |
| GA → FL | Southbound | Strong hospital supply chain, consistent volume |
| SoCal → NorCal | Northbound | Intrastate CA; medical supply from LA region to Bay Area |
| Carolinas → Northeast | Northbound | Medical device manufacturing in NC/SC, demand in NY/NJ/PA |

## Avoid or Scrutinize

| Lane | Reason |
|---|---|
| Anything → FL or FL → Anything | **IRON RULE — hard reject** |
| Cross-mountain routes (CO, WY in winter) | Weather delays, driver issues |
| Rural-only lanes with no backhaul options | Carrier won't have return freight |
| Lanes where average RPM is below $2.75 | Not worth the effort |

## Lane Scoring (How Erin Evaluates a New Lane)

When a load comes in on a lane Erin hasn't seen before:

1. Check: Is origin or destination Florida? → Reject immediately
2. Check: What's the typical RPM for this lane? (Use DAT rate history)
3. Check: Are there backhaul options? (Carrier will want return freight)
4. Check: How many of our carriers serve this lane?
5. Check: Is there a medical facility cluster at destination? (Higher chance of repeat business)

Erin saves every new lane to memory with its average RPM and volume score.
Over time she builds her own lane intelligence database.
