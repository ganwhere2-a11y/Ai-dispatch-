# Workshop: Converting Free Trial Carriers to Paying Clients

## What You'll Learn
How the 7-day free trial works end-to-end, what makes a trial succeed or fail, how to handle the Day 7 conversion conversation, and how to respond to the most common objections carriers raise when you ask them to sign.

---

## The Lesson

**Why offer a free trial at all?**
Carriers are skeptical of dispatchers. They've been burned — dispatchers who don't find good loads, take too high a cut, or go silent after the first load. The 7-day free trial removes the risk for them. You prove your value first. If you find them quality loads in 7 days, the conversion conversation practically writes itself.

**The Trial Flow:**

```
Day 0: Carrier signs up → Onboarding verifies MC#, insurance, authority age, safety rating
       → If passes: Welcome email sent, Erin starts dispatching for them immediately
       → If fails: Polite rejection with reason

Days 1-4: Erin dispatches real loads for the carrier. Every load is logged.
          → Loads found, rates, miles, estimated profit — all tracked in Airtable
          → Carrier gets real dispatch service, real paperwork, real support

Day 5: Marketer sends the Trial Recap email
       → Shows exactly what was delivered: X loads found, $X in potential revenue,
          X hours Erin worked their account, comparison to what they were making before
       → Soft CTA: "We'd love to keep working together"

Day 7: Onboarding sends the Conversion Offer email
       → Clear ask: sign the agreement, lock in your rate
       → Two outcomes: CONVERTED → contract + Airtable update + first paid load
                       NOT CONVERTED → 30-day nurture drip (4 more emails)
```

**The Commission Rate:**
- They sign within the trial or immediately after: **8%** (existing carrier rate)
- They come back 3+ months later: still 8% for the first 3 months, then 10%
- New carriers signing after referral: 10% from day one

**What Erin does during the trial:**
Exactly what she does for paid carriers. No reduced service. Full dispatch, full paperwork, full compliance checks. This is the product demo — it has to be real.

## The Conversion Conversation

**Day 7 email structure (the offer):**
1. Open with results: "In 7 days, we found you X loads worth $X in revenue."
2. Show the value: "That's X hours of load searching you didn't have to do."
3. Make the ask: "We'd like to keep this going. Here's what it looks like."
4. Remove friction: "The agreement is one page. Commission is 8% — you only pay when we find you a load."
5. Clear CTA: "Click here to sign" or "Reply YES and I'll send the DocuSign link."

**The math you show them:**
```
8% commission on a $2,000 load = $160 you pay
But you got a $2,000 load without spending 3 hours searching
At $50/hr value of your time: that's $150 in time saved
Net cost to you: $10 for a $2,000 payday
```

## Common Objections and Responses

**Objection 1: "The commission is too high."**
Response: "I understand. 8% is $X on an average load for your lanes. What that buys you is: load searching, carrier-shipper communication, all the paperwork, compliance checks, and someone watching your truck every day. What were you paying before in time and stress? Most of our carriers say it pays for itself on the first load."

**Objection 2: "I want to try another dispatcher first."**
Response: "That's completely fair. Take your time — you should work with who's best for you. I'll leave the door open. If the other dispatcher doesn't work out, reach out and we'll pick up where we left off. Your FMCSA record stays in our system."

**Objection 3: "Can you lower the commission to 6%?"**
Response: "I can't — 8% is the floor and it's the same for all our carriers. What I can do is make sure the loads I'm finding for you justify the cost. If you're not making more than you're paying us, we're doing something wrong." (Never negotiate the commission down.)

**Objection 4: "I only want to pay after I get paid."**
Response: "Agreed — that's exactly how it works. You don't pay anything until the load delivers and the shipper pays. Our commission comes out of the carrier rate, which we collect. You never write us a check."

**Objection 5: "What if you can't find me loads?"**
Response: "Then you don't pay us anything — no loads, no commission. The agreement doesn't lock you in to minimums. If we're not finding you quality loads, you can cancel with 30 days notice."

## Practice Questions

**Question 1:** A carrier ran 3 loads during their trial worth a total of $4,200 in carrier rates. What was the potential commission you earned (at 8%)? What would it be at 10%?

*Expected: 8%: $336. 10%: $420.*

**Question 2:** A carrier says "I only need a dispatcher in winter — can I just use you seasonally?"
How do you respond?

*Expected: Yes — the agreement doesn't require a minimum volume. Seasonal is fine. They can use you when they need loads and the 30-day cancellation means they have flexibility. This is actually a low-risk way to get them signed.*

**Question 3:** A trial carrier finds good loads on their own during the trial week and says "I don't really need a dispatcher — I found loads myself." How do you respond?

*Expected: Acknowledge it — they're capable. Then reframe: the value isn't that they can't find loads, it's that they shouldn't have to. Ask: "How many hours did you spend finding those loads? What else could you have done with that time?" The dispatcher is a force multiplier, not a crutch.*

## Mastery Check

A carrier just finished their 7-day trial. Results:
- 4 loads found by Erin
- Total carrier rates: $6,800
- Carrier picked up 3 of the 4 (declined one due to route preference)
- Zero compliance issues
- Carrier response to Day 5 email: "Pretty good so far, we'll see"

It's Day 7. Write the key talking points for your conversion email. What do you lead with? What objection should you pre-handle?

*Expected:*
*Lead with: the 3 loads picked up = $6,120 in revenue. At 8% that's $490 in commission — roughly $163/load. Show the ROI.*
*Pre-handle the likely objection: "We'll see" language suggests price sensitivity. Preemptively show the math: $163 per load is less than 2 hours of the carrier's time at typical hourly value.*
*Close: one page agreement, 30-day cancellation, no minimums, 8% only when loads deliver.*
