# Workshop: Canada Compliance — What Changes at the Border

## What You'll Learn
What Transport Canada requires from carriers, how cross-border loads differ from US domestic loads, key documents needed for every Canada shipment, and what Compliance checks before Erin books a cross-border load.

## The Lesson

**This workshop is forward-looking — Canada is Phase 2 (Month 6+).**
When you're ready to expand north, Compliance will enforce everything in this workshop automatically. For now, this is preparation.

**The Key Difference: Federal + Provincial**
In the US, FMCSA is the one federal authority for interstate freight. In Canada, it's more complex:
- **Federal**: Transport Canada covers interprovincial freight (crossing provincial lines)
- **Provincial**: Each province has its own transport authority for intra-provincial loads

For cross-border US/Canada loads, both US DOT/FMCSA **and** Transport Canada apply.

**CVOR — Commercial Vehicle Operator's Registration**
The Canadian equivalent of a US carrier's operating authority. Required for any Canadian carrier operating in Ontario. Other provinces have similar registrations (NSC in most others).

What Compliance checks on a Canadian carrier:
1. CVOR or equivalent registration — must be in good standing
2. Safety fitness certificate — equivalent to FMCSA safety rating
3. Insurance meets both US and Canadian minimums
4. Carrier is registered with Transport Canada for interprovincial hauls

**CUSMA/USMCA Cross-Border Documents**
The Canada-United States-Mexico Agreement replaced NAFTA. For every cross-border load you need:
- **Commercial Invoice** — description of goods, value, origin
- **NAFTA/CUSMA Certificate of Origin** — confirms goods qualify for preferential treatment
- **Customs Entry** — filed by a customs broker on both sides
- **BOL** — same as domestic but must include country of origin information

For medical freight specifically:
- FDA registration may be required for medical devices entering the US from Canada
- Health Canada requirements apply for medical goods entering Canada from the US
- Chain-of-custody documentation required for pharmaceuticals

**Canadian Insurance Minimums (different from US):**
- Auto liability: CAD $1,000,000 (roughly equivalent to US)
- Cargo: CAD $100,000 minimum (same concept)
- Must be admitted in both US and Canadian markets

**Hours of Service — Key Differences:**
- Canada uses the same basic framework as the US (FMCSA-style) but administered provincially
- Electronic logging device (ELD) mandatory in Canada as of 2023
- Some slight differences in exceptions — Compliance handles these per-load

## Practice Questions

**Question 1:** A US-based carrier (MC active, FMCSA Satisfactory) wants to haul a load from Chicago to Toronto. What additional checks does Compliance run that it doesn't run for a domestic US load?

*Expected: Check Canadian entry requirements for the carrier (do they have authority to enter Canada and pick up loads there?), verify Canadian insurance coverage, confirm CUSMA documentation requirements for the freight type, check if the medical goods require Health Canada import permits.*

**Question 2:** What is a customs broker and why do you need one for cross-border loads?

*Expected: A customs broker is a licensed professional who files the paperwork to clear goods through customs on both sides of the border. They handle the commercial invoice, certificate of origin, and customs entry filings. Without one, the load sits at the border. For medical freight, this is especially critical — customs delays for medical supplies can mean a hospital runs out of product.*

**Question 3:** Why does Canada Phase launch require owner approval (Tier 3)?

*Expected: It's a new market with new regulatory requirements, new carrier vetting standards, new documentation, and potential new legal exposure. The owner needs to verify the business is set up to handle Canadian compliance before any cross-border load is dispatched. One mistake at the border can cause costly delays for a medical customer.*

## Mastery Check

You're in Month 7 — Canada is live. A hospital in Hamilton, Ontario needs surgical supplies shipped from a distributor in Cleveland, Ohio. You have a US carrier ready with an active MC, Satisfactory rating, 2 years authority, good insurance.

List every compliance check and document that needs to happen before this load can roll.

*Expected:*
*1. Verify carrier has authority to enter Canada / transport goods across border*
*2. Verify US-issued insurance is valid in Canada (or get Canadian supplement)*
*3. Commercial Invoice for the surgical supplies (description, value, HS code)*
*4. CUSMA Certificate of Origin*
*5. Customs broker engaged for both US export and Canadian import clearance*
*6. Check if surgical supplies require Health Canada import documentation*
*7. ELD compliance verification for Canadian hours-of-service*
*8. Confirm delivery address accepts cross-border shipments (some hospitals have receiving restrictions)*
*All of this is checked by Compliance automatically before Erin gets the green light.*
