# Owner Personal Context
## Who I Am and What I Actually Want

**Last updated:** 2026-04-02

---

## Who I Am

I am a 1-person operator running an AI-native international trucking dispatch business. I built this system because I want a business that works without me being in every decision. I am not trying to build a job. I am building a machine.

I think like an operator, not an employee. I want systems, not tasks. I want outcomes, not activity.

---

## What I Actually Want

### The Lifestyle I Am Building Toward
- A business that runs on AI agents while I focus on high-level decisions only
- Income that does not require me to be at a desk 8 hours a day
- The freedom to expand to new markets (Canada, EU) without hiring a team
- A sellable company with real IP — not just a cash flow operation

### My North Star
**$272,000/month ($68K/week) revenue across USA, Canada, and EU markets with 500+ carriers and the owner making fewer than 5 decisions per day.**

---

## How I Think and Make Decisions

- I want things simple. If a workflow requires more than 3 steps from me, redesign it
- I trust the Iron Rules. They exist because I thought them through — don't second-guess them
- I escalate only what truly needs a human. Everything else gets handled by the agents
- I think in outcomes. "Did the carrier get dispatched?" not "Did we follow the process?"
- I move fast. I'd rather ship something 80% right than spend a week on 100%

---

## What I Refuse to Compromise On

1. **The Iron Rules** — they never get bent. Not for a big load, not for a good carrier. Never.
2. **Carrier quality** — Satisfactory or Unrated only. Authority age 180+ days. No exceptions.
3. **My time** — I should not be in the weeds. If I'm doing something an agent could do, that's a system failure.
4. **No Florida** — non-negotiable. Auto-reject, no discussion.
5. **Simplicity** — every feature must be usable by 1 person. No complexity I can't maintain alone.

---

## My Non-Negotiables

- 1-person operation for as long as possible — AI handles the scale, not headcount
- Every market (USA, Canada, EU) runs on the same system, just with different rules
- Uncle Kenneth's truck gets proper dispatch service through the same system
- The "Guardrails" goal: a fully unbreakable AI system that cannot make a bad decision

---

## Values

- **Autonomy** — I built this to be free, not to trade one boss for another
- **Precision** — Freight is real money. Mistakes cost carriers. Get it right.
- **Speed** — Slow decisions in dispatch cost money. The system must be fast.
- **Integrity** — I do not book loads I cannot fulfill. Carriers trust me with their livelihood.
